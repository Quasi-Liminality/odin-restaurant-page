About Odin's

Step into a slice of culinary eternity! Established circa 3,200 BCE in the glorious city of Uruk, where civilization itself was born, Odin's has been serving humanity (and the occasional deity) for over five millennia. That’s right—this is not just a restaurant. This is history on a plate.

Our Origins

Founded by the legendary Enheduanna the Elder (self-declared "First Restaurateur of Humanity"), Odin's began as a humble tavern catering to scribes fresh out of cuneiform practice. Legend has it that Enheduanna invented hummus here, inspired by a divine dream sent by Inanna herself. Over the centuries, we’ve expanded our menu and polished our craft—one Mesopotamian mezze platter at a time.

Our Commitment

From the first amphora of beer lovingly served to Gilgamesh himself, to the exquisite lamb kebabs you’ll enjoy today, our mission has always been the same: legendary service, epic meals, and zero complaints (except that one time during the Babylonian captivity… but we don’t talk about that).
